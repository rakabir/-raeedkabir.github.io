Charitable Giving in the Face of Uncertainty
1
How Does Uncertainty about the Future Shape Charitable Giving?
Working paper with Michael Price and Tigran Melkonyan.
Working Paper
Presented at: Advances with Field Experiments, Harvard Economics Pre-Doc Seminar, University of Alabama Brown Bag, University of Alabama Capstone Scholars Day.
We extend the DellaVigna, List and Malmendier (2012) model of charitable giving to an alpha-maximin representation that allows for ambiguity over future income streams. We show that beliefs about future income can impact giving through two potential channels: (i) changes in the marginal cost of giving and (ii) changes in expectations over the gifts of others and, hence, the norms that underlie social pressures. We test key predictions of the model using two distinct data sources. The first data set relates macro-uncertainty beliefs to aggregate giving in the United States. The second data set relates macro-uncertainty to the likelihood that an individual has donated to a charitable cause in the past month. Consistent with our model, we find that greater macroeconomic uncertainty leads to reductions in both aggregate giving in the United States and a lower propensity to have donated to at least one charitable cause in the past month.
